About Year Up

Located on 80 Sutter Street San Francisco, CA, Year Up Bay Area is changing the lives of hundreds of young professionals each year. Year Up is giving individuals with less opportunities a chance at higher education and a chance to earn an internship through their many corporate partners. How are they doing this? Keep reading and you will find out!

Two Phase System

At Year Up, trainees embark upon a two phase system. The first system is called Learning and Development. In this phase, trainees are taught Business Communications, Professional Skills, Computer Hardware and Software, and depending on which track students are placed into, they are also taught IT Support, Project Management, Quality Assurance, and Cyber Security. In the second phase, trainees will have earned their internship through demonstrations during Learning and Development. This is where trainees will gain valuable corporate experience so that they can land a career after the program.

Overview of Learning and Development

As stated above, with the Learning and Devlopment phase, trainees are introduced to a number and communication and technical trainings. These trainings are separated into 3 "Modules" which are 7-week sprints. The first and second modules are very similar due to the fact that all of the trainees learn fundamentals of Computer Science and Communications which consist of Computer Hardware/Software, Networking, Business Communications, and Professional Skills. Furthermore, it is in the Learning and Development phase where trainees create and be a part of a community Year Up is famous for.
